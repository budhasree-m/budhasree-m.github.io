Methodology — Scenario-Based Estimation: Using T = R / p, three scenarios are projected simultaneously. p = 0.3 (conservative) — only 30% of crimes are reported, consistent with high-stigma crime research. p = 0.5 (moderate) — 50% reporting. p = 0.7 (optimistic) — 70% reporting. Regression Residuals — Where Data Defies Expectation
OLS: crime rate ~ log(population) · residual = actual − predicted · negative may signal underreporting
Method: OLS regression predicts each state's crime rate from log-transformed population. Negative residual = records less than population predicts — possible underreporting or social barriers to filing FIRs. Positive residual = records more than predicted — better reporting infrastructure, genuine hotspots, or higher institutional capacity.
OLS uses population as sole predictor — simplified proxy. Future versions will add literacy, female LFPR, and police strength per lakh to build a proper Visibility Index.
// 03 · Methodology & Transparency

Every Assumption
Made Explicit

Model 01 · Scenario Estimation
T = R / p

Applied at p = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7. Produces a plausible range, not a single true number. p is held constant within each scenario and does not vary by state or crime type. Best used for visualising the potential scale of underreporting.

Model 02 · Regression Residuals
Actual − Predicted

OLS regression: crime rate ~ log(population). Residuals flag states deviating from population-predicted levels. Current predictor is population only. Future iterations add literacy, police strength, female LFPR, and GDP per capita.

Data Sources
What Feeds These Charts

NCRB Crime in India 2023 — national time series 1981–2023 and Table 1A (state-wise IPC crimes 2021–2023). Population: NCRB mid-year projected 2023 figures. No NFHS-5 or auxiliary socio-demographic data in this version.

What this project does not claim: These visualizations do not assert a definitive dark figure. They demonstrate that silence in official data does not mean absence of crime. All models are transparent, assumptions are stated, and uncertainty is shown — not hidden. Next phase: NFHS-5 domestic violence prevalence, state literacy rates, police strength per lakh, and female LFPR to build a proper Visibility Index quadrant scatterplot.
